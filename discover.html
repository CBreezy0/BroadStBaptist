Welcome to Broad Street Missionary Baptist Church

We're glad you're taking time to discover who we are! Broad Street has been a cornerstone of faith in Historic Birmingham for over a century, and we'd love to share our story, values, and vision with you.

Our Story

Broad Street Missionary Baptist Church has a rich heritage rooted in faith, community, and service. Our journey began in 1878 as Spring Street Baptist Church, and through God's providence, we found our permanent home in 1909 at our current location.

1878 - The Beginning

Spring Street Baptist Church was organized by faithful believers who gathered in a small frame house donated by Mr. Felix Mudd.

1909 - New Home

The church moved to its present location and was renamed Broad Street Missionary Baptist Church, using bricks donated from the old Elyton Courthouse.

1920-1958 - Growth & Development

Under Rev. J.H. Holmes' 38-year leadership, the church flourished with major renovations, new pews, carpet, and a Hammond organ installation.

2024 - New Leadership

Rev. Rick (Rickey) Owens was called as pastor, continuing our legacy of faithful ministry in Historic Birmingham.

Our Values

As a Baptist church, we hold to fundamental Christian truths that have guided believers for centuries:

Scripture Authority

We believe the Bible (KJV) is the inspired, inerrant Word of God and our final authority for faith and practice.

Salvation by Grace

Salvation comes through faith in Jesus Christ alone, not by works, and is a gift of God's grace.

Baptism by Immersion

We practice baptism by immersion as a public declaration of faith for those who have accepted Christ.

Priesthood of Believers

Every believer has direct access to God and the responsibility to serve in ministry.

Church Autonomy

Each local church is independent and self-governing under the lordship of Jesus Christ.

Congregational Government

Church decisions are made democratically by the congregation under pastoral leadership.

What Makes Us Unique

Broad Street Missionary Baptist Church is distinguished by:

  • Rich Heritage: Deep roots in the Birmingham community since 1878.
  • Committed Leadership: Dedicated pastors and leaders who care for our congregation.
  • Active Ministries: Numerous programs serving all ages and needs.
  • Community Focus: Strong emphasis on outreach and service in Birmingham.
  • Welcoming Atmosphere: A friendly, inclusive environment for worship and fellowship.
