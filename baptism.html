Baptism

Your Public Declaration of Faith in Christ

Celebrating Your Faith Journey

Baptism is one of the most significant steps in a believer's faith journey. It's a public declaration of your personal relationship with Jesus Christ and your commitment to follow Him. At Broad Street, we're honored to help you take this important step of obedience.

Biblical Significance

Baptism is not just a church traditionâ€”it's a biblical command that Jesus Himself followed and instructed His followers to observe. Scripture teaches us about the importance and meaning of this sacred act:

"Therefore we are buried with him by baptism into death: that like as Christ was raised up from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life."

Romans 6:4 KJV

What Baptism Represents:

  • Death to Sin: Going under the water symbolizes dying to your old life of sin
  • Burial with Christ: Being submerged represents being buried with Christ
  • Resurrection: Coming up out of the water symbolizes your new life in Christ
  • Public Declaration: It's your public testimony of faith before the church and community
  • Obedience: Following Christ's example and command to be baptized

Who Can Be Baptized

At Broad Street Missionary Baptist Church, we practice believer's baptism, which means baptism is for those who have made a personal decision to trust Jesus Christ as their Lord and Savior.

Requirements for Baptism:

  • Personal Faith: You must have personally accepted Jesus Christ as your Savior
  • Understanding: You should understand what baptism represents and why you want to be baptized
  • Public Decision: You should be ready to make a public declaration of your faith
  • Age Consideration: While there's no specific age requirement, candidates should be old enough to understand the significance of their decision

Not Sure If You're Ready?

If you're unsure about baptism or have questions about salvation, we encourage you to speak with Pastor Owens or one of our ministry leaders. We're here to help you understand what it means to follow Christ and ensure you're ready for this important step.

Scheduling Your Baptism:

  • Contact the church office to express your desire to be baptized.
  • Meet with Pastor Owens for a baptism consultation.
  • Attend a baptism preparation class (if required).
  • Schedule your baptism date during a worship service.
  • Invite family and friends to witness your baptism.
